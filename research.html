Ringdown

Black hole spectroscopy

When two black holes merge, the remnant settles down by emitting gravitational waves at a discrete set of complex frequencies — its quasinormal modes. These frequencies depend only on the mass and spin of the final black hole, making ringdown a clean test of the Kerr hypothesis. We study how these modes are excited, how their amplitudes encode the initial data, and how to extract them reliably from numerical and observed signals.
