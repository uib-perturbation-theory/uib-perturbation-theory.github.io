We listen to the ringing of spacetime.

We study black holes, gravitational waves and strong-field gravity — from the mathematics of perturbed spacetimes to the signals measured by LIGO, Virgo and the detectors of the next decade.

What we do

Black holes as laboratories for fundamental physics

01 · Theory

Black hole perturbation theory

Quasinormal modes, ringdown spectroscopy and the excitation of black hole resonances, connecting initial data to the signal an observer measures.

02 · Waveforms

Gravitational-wave modelling

Accurate waveform models for compact binary coalescences, from inspiral through merger and ringdown, ready for use in detection and parameter estimation.

03 · Data

Tests of general relativity

Confronting theory with LIGO–Virgo–KAGRA observations to test the Kerr hypothesis and search for physics beyond general relativity.
