Interactive gravity

Black Hole Lab

Six small animations to explain black-hole physics visually: ringdown, accretion, lensing, spacetime distortion, binary coalescence and interactive perturbations.

01 · Ringdown

Quasinormal-mode ringing

A perturbed black hole relaxes like a struck bell. The shadow is deformed in a quadrupolar pattern and the deformation decays with the Schwarzschild fundamental-mode ratio ωR/ωI ≈ 4.2.

02 · Accretion

Relativistic beaming

The inclined disk is brighter on the approaching side and dimmer on the receding side. It is not a full ray-traced simulation, but it captures the basic visual idea behind Doppler boosting.

03 · Lensing

Starfield lensing

Background stars are displaced and stretched around the shadow, hinting at primary and secondary images near an Einstein ring.

04 · Shader

The page bends around the hole

A WebGL fragment shader bends a texture as if the black hole were distorting the light from the page itself. Move the cursor over the panel to drag the lens.

05 · Binary coalescence

Inspiral, merger and ringdown

Two compact objects inspiral, merge and leave behind a ringing remnant. The strain strip below the animation changes from a chirp to a damped sinusoid.

06 · Interactive perturbation

Poke the black hole

Use the cursor as a passing star. Moving close to the hole or clicking on the panel excites a damped quadrupolar oscillation and launches gravitational-wave ripples.
